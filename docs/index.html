How this was computed & caveats (read me)

Source. Parsed directly from missions/*.txt and map/{area,region,superregion}.txt in your EU4 install, with all DLC enabled. Legacy pre-DLC mission trees (hidden because you own the newer DLC) are excluded.

  • Missions = total missions across every active, tag-locked mission series that nation's tag can see.
  • Core regions = regions targeted by enough missions to be a real objective (weight ≥ max(3, 12% of the nation's missions)). Used for conflict detection.
  • A "clean" matchup = (1) zero shared core regions, (2) no shared mission series (not the same lineage/formable chain — so you'll never see e.g. Castile vs Spain), and (3) similar size (smaller/larger ≥ 0.72, or within 12 missions).
  • Overlap = number of regions both footprints touch at all (incl. peripheral/sea zones). 0 = fully disjoint.
  • F = formable: you must form the nation in-game before its tree unlocks — a time cost, not a 1444 start.
  • Well-characterised = both sides have ≥2 detected core regions (a confidence filter; nomadic/colonial trees like the Hordes or Portugal spread too thin to pin down and may give looser "non-conflicting" claims).

Size ≠ difficulty. The files encode mission count (a good race-length proxy) but not how hard a tree is. Judge difficulty yourselves. A few nations (e.g. Mughals, Ming) use unusual potential patterns and are under-counted here.
